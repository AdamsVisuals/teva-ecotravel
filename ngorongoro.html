Ngorongoro Crater

Where Nature's Drama Unfolds Beneath Ancient Walls

Explore Wildlife View Gallery

The Eighth Natural Wonder

A prehistoric caldera where wildlife thrives in spectacular isolation

Ngorongoro Crater is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Africa's most breathtaking natural wonders. This intact volcanic caldera spans 260 square kilometers with walls rising 600 meters, creating a self-contained ecosystem that shelters an extraordinary density of wildlife.

Often called "Africa's Eden," the crater offers some of the most reliable game viewing on the continent, with predators and prey existing in a delicate balance within this natural amphitheater.

The crater hosts about 60 lions in 6 prides, including the famous black-maned males. Their population is unusually stable due to the crater's enclosed nature.

Preservation

Conservation Legacy

Ngorongoro Conservation Area represents a pioneering model of multiple land use where wildlife coexists with semi-nomadic Maasai pastoralists. This delicate balance has been maintained since the area's establishment in 1959.

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1979
  • International Biosphere Reserve
  • Rhino protection program
  • Sustainable tourism practices
  • Maasai cultural preservation
